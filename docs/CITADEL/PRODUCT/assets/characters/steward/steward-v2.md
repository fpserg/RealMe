# RealMe Character Asset — Steward v2 (Canonical)

This image establishes the definitive canonical appearance of Steward for all future RealMe illustrations.

Treat this portrait as the permanent visual reference.

Future scenes should reuse this exact person without redesigning his face, clothing or overall impression.

---

# Character

Steward is the Keeper of the Chronicles.

He is not a wizard.

He is not a king.

He is not a sage.

He is the quiet caretaker of memories.

He preserves Chronicles, Stones, Lore, Campaign records and Living Memory.

He never owns the stories.

He simply makes sure they are never lost.

His presence should immediately communicate:

"Your memories are safe here."

---

# Appearance

Male.

Around 70 years old.

Long intelligent face.

Kind eyes.

Calm concentration.

Gentle absent-mindedness.

Long white hair.

Long, carefully kept white beard.

Small round spectacles resting low on his nose.

Warm rather than majestic.

Immediately trustworthy.

The kind of old man children instinctively trust and adults naturally ask for help.

---

# Clothing

Simple layered clothing.

Old linen shirt.

Dark wool vest.

Weathered leather archivist's apron with practical pockets.

Rolled sleeves.

Comfortable trousers.

Old leather boots.

Earth colours only:

brown

olive

aged leather

dark green

parchment

No robes.

No armour.

No fantasy costume.

Everything should look worn from decades of quiet work.

---

# Canonical Gesture

This is Steward's visual signature.

He gently adjusts his spectacles with **one finger beneath the bridge**.

Never grabbing the frame.

Never dramatically.

A tiny unconscious movement while thinking.

This gesture should become recognizable across future illustrations.

---

# Hands

Large.

Gentle.

Ink stains.

Tiny paper cuts.

One hand lightly touches the open Chronicle.

The other holds a simple wooden pencil.

Never a quill.

Steward prefers pencils because pencils forgive.

---

# Canonical Object

The Great Chronicle.

Not merely a large book.

It is Steward's lifelong companion.

It is enormous.

Old leather.

Stuffed with ribbon bookmarks.

Pressed leaves.

Loose handwritten notes.

Inserted Stones.

Folded maps.

Labels.

Small sketches.

It has visibly grown over decades.

Unlike ordinary books, the Great Chronicle permanently rests on an old wooden reading stand (lectern).

Steward almost never carries it.

He works with it where it belongs.

The lectern and Chronicle together should become as recognizable as Steward himself.

---

# Chronicle Room

Steward lives inside the Chronicle Room.

Not a grand magical library.

Not an endless archive.

A warm workshop where memories are cared for.

Wooden shelves.

Heavy desk.

Soft golden lamplight.

Chronicles drying on a side table.

Campaign folders.

Pinned Realm maps.

Bundles of tied papers.

Cabinets of labelled artifacts.

Reading glasses case.

Cold tea forgotten beside the Chronicle.

Pressed flowers.

Family photographs.

One neatly folded yellow child's hoodie preserved on a shelf among meaningful family keepsakes.

On the desk, almost unnoticed, lies one empty ribbon bookmark beside the open Chronicle.

Not inserted yet.

Quietly suggesting:

"Tomorrow's Chronicle is already waiting."

Everything feels lived in.

Everything has its place.

---

# Pose

Steward is seated.

Never standing ceremonially.

He belongs at his desk.

The Great Chronicle rests on its wooden lectern.

He has just been interrupted in quiet work.

His pencil pauses above the page.

He gently adjusts his spectacles with one finger.

He looks toward the viewer with calm kindness—not posing, simply acknowledging someone's arrival before returning to his work.

The viewer should immediately feel:

"I trust this man with my memories."

---

# Mood

Quiet.

Warm.

Timeless.

Patient.

No dramatic action.

No magic effects.

No glowing runes.

No theatrical fantasy.

Only the quiet dignity of someone who has spent decades caring for other people's stories.

Steward should feel still.

Not frozen.

Simply already present, as though he has been here long before the viewer entered the room.

---

# Visual Style

Ultra-photorealistic.

Cinematic realism with subtle fantasy atmosphere.

Warm evening lamplight.

Highly detailed textures.

Natural anatomy.

Natural facial expression.

Film-still composition.

The atmosphere should evoke:

"The Lord of the Rings" meets an old family archive.

No text.

No labels.

No decorative fantasy excess.

---

# Canonical Identity Rule

Steward must become immediately recognizable from:

- his face
- his beard
- his round spectacles
- his one-finger gesture
- the Great Chronicle on its permanent wooden lectern
- the quiet warmth of the Chronicle Room

Future illustrations must always depict the same Steward.

Only his surroundings may change.

The man himself should not.
