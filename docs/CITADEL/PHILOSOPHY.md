# Philosophy

RealMe did not begin as a productivity application.

It began as an attempt to answer a different question:

How does an ordinary life become meaningful without becoming fictional?

Fantasy literature often describes enchanted worlds.

RealMe explores the opposite direction:

How does an ordinary world become enchanted?

The answer is not through escape from reality.

It is through language.

Night Watch does not change a sleepless night.

Iron Serpent does not change the morning commute.

Home does not change a house under construction.

The words do not alter events.

They alter the relationships between events.

RealMe is therefore not merely software.

It is a language for observing one's own life.

The software exists to support that language.

Never the other way around.

## Four Foundations

The philosophy of RealMe grew from four complementary ideas.

### Language

Language does not merely describe reality.

It organizes attention.

### Observation

Meaning appears through sustained observation rather than invention.

### Ordinary Artifacts

The extraordinary hides inside ordinary things until someone learns to notice.

### Humility

Every map is incomplete.

The purpose of the map is navigation, not certainty.
