# 🌳 Day 022 — Roots and Storms

---

## 🌅 Morning Orientation

The morning began with three practical paths through three different parts of the map.

### 🏡 Household → Stronghold

The Stronghold was running short of drinking water, so the Warden and Owling set out on a routine Supplies Quest.

The Scroll contained:

- ✅ Drinking water
- ✅ Toilet paper

Kadyr continued advancing the Bathroom Campaign.

### 🏰 Third Realm → Tower

Wednesday meant preparing the Tower dividend report.

The Warden hoped to rest while Owling slept, but rest gradually transformed into gathering and organizing Tower data.

### 💼 Career Realm

Career initially remained quiet.

It would not stay that way.

---

## 🛒 The Noble Water and Toilet Paper Mission

The Supplies Quest was completed without interference from dragons, clerks or unknown documents.

The Stronghold would neither thirst nor suffer an avoidable domestic crisis.

Unlike the Nightmare-class Paper Dragon Campaign, this was a Routine quest.

The distinction matters.

---

## 🦆 A Pond Covered with Duckweed

On the way back, Owling became furious for no obvious reason.

Amid the wild yelling, the Warden selfishly slowed down for several seconds to preserve a RealMe moment:

A duck leading her ducklings across a pond covered with duckweed.

A small apology was left for Future Owling:

> Sorry, Owling, if you happen to read this one day.
>
> And yes, during the summer of 2026 I called you Owling because of the sleepless nights we spent together.

A quick nod was exchanged with the Wooden Guardian.

The proposed nickname “Woody” was immediately rejected as far too unserious for such an ancient figure.

Then the Playground Quest continued.

---

## 🐉 Owling’s Booba Season

Owling arrived at the Playground wearing Jr’s old Dragon hoodie.

Another piece of childhood clothing had quietly passed from one brother to another.

His favourite cartoon at the time was **Booba**—the small white creature whose curiosity turns almost every new place into a disaster.

The resemblance was difficult to ignore.

Owling’s first clearly distinguishable word had not been “mummy” or “daddy.”

It had been:

> **Booba.**

Like his favourite character, Owling approached the world through curiosity rather than caution.

Every unfamiliar object demanded investigation.

Things occasionally came apart.

Sometimes he tried to put them back together.

---

## 🗼 The Playground Water Tower

As usual, Owling adopted an object that did not belong to him but temporarily became the most important artifact in the world.

Today it was the Playground’s toy water tower.

Soon it came apart in his hands.

Owling bent over the pieces and studied them with great concentration, trying to understand how they fitted together.

The Dragon was not upset.

Only deeply occupied with repairing the little tower he had just dismantled.

---

## 🏰 The Plastic Stronghold

Another Playground artifact appeared: a small red plastic castle.

The Warden held the miniature Stronghold in the foreground while Owling energetically threw toys out of the sandbox behind it.

Castles are built.

Booba investigates.

Sometimes those are opposite activities.

---

## 🌿 Claimed Artifact of the Day

Every Playground visit seems to produce one temporarily adopted treasure.

Today’s claimed artifact was:

> **A tiny green plastic rake.**

It was carried and examined with complete seriousness and, for a while, became the most important tool in the entire Playground.

---

## 🔥 The Great Boiler Hall

Owling’s favourite room inside Stronghold had also revealed itself.

Adults call it the boiler room.

Owling knows it as:

> **The Great Boiler Hall.**

It contains pipes, valves, gauges, tanks, handles and mysterious machinery.

Every lever asks to be moved.

Every pipe deserves inspection.

Every button represents an unanswered question.

Its attraction is strengthened by one important property:

> Owling is not allowed to enter.

Like many explorers before him, he is irresistibly drawn toward doors marked *Do Not Enter*.

---

## 🌳 Dirt Digger Quest Unlocked

The Gardening Campaign moved from plans and purchases into the soil itself.

Its first permanent Stone was planted:

# **Sir Maple**

The Warden dug the pit and completed his part of the planting.

Oksi then fertilized and prepared the soil while Jr stood beside the young tree, holding Sir Maple upright against the breeze.

Meanwhile, the Warden moved directly into another quest:

rocking Owling to sleep.

Thus the first named resident of the future Garden took root.

---

## 🌲 The Thuja Sisters

The Warden briefly assumed that Sir Maple would be the only Gardening Stone of the day.

This assumption failed to account for Captain Jack Sparrow.

More holes had to be dug.

Before the day was over, two of the **Thuja Sisters** were already planted and sipping water from Stronghold soil.

The remaining sisters waited for the Captain to return from another fertilizer expedition.

The Garden of the Nest had unmistakably begun.

### Garden Campaign — Milestone I

- ✅ Sir Maple
- ✅ Thuja Sister I
- ✅ Thuja Sister II
- ⏳ Remaining Thuja Sisters
- ⏳ Landscape leveling
- ⏳ Drainage
- ⏳ Lawn
- ⏳ Future plantings
- ⏳ Fence

No badge was awarded.

Badges commemorate completed chapters, not their opening pages.

Sir Maple nevertheless earned XP as the first permanent Stone of the Garden Campaign.

---

## ☠️ Battle with Hydraweed

While the shovel was already in his hands, the Warden launched a quick attack against a dangerous enemy growing behind the future Dream Garden.

Its ordinary name was giant hogweed.

Its Stronghold name became:

> **Hydraweed.**

The plants had been poisoned the previous year.

Many fell.

Some survived.

This year they returned.

Like a hydra, defeating one generation did not end the campaign.

The battle remains active.

---

## 🛠 Construction Market Expedition

There was still no proper rest.

Kadyr required drills, so the Warden drove him to the construction market and back.

The Bathroom Campaign continued advancing while the Garden Campaign expanded around it.

---

## 💼 An Unexpected Career Summons

Career Realm then broke into the day without warning.

The Warden was summoned to a Client Call Quest.

The day had already included:

- Stronghold supplies
- Playground expeditions
- Tower report preparation
- tree planting
- thuja planting
- Hydraweed combat
- contractor logistics

Yet Career still demanded its share of attention.

The Realms rarely respect one another’s borders.

---

## 🐺 Night Watch — The Battle for Sirius

The outer and inner storms returned at night.

While Oksi was planting bushes, Sirius walked across the newly prepared ground.

She became angry, kicked him and said that he would be chained.

The Warden intervened, describing permanent chaining as cruel and arguing that Labradors are deeply social dogs who need to remain close to their humans.

The disagreement quickly expanded beyond Sirius into another familiar House of Quarrels.

Harsh words returned.

The Warden later searched the question independently and found that many sources indeed discourage keeping Labradors permanently chained because of their social nature and emotional attachment to people.

This did not resolve the conflict.

The Chronicle records the event without attempting to turn it into a verdict.

On this night, Sirius stood at the center of a larger storm.

---

## 🌩 Stronghold Becomes Stormhold

The weather outside mirrored the weather within.

Rain continued through the night.

The Household storm grew worse rather than better.

For one week of outer thunderstorms, inner quarrels and unfinished campaigns, Stronghold briefly earned a temporary name:

> **Stormhold.**

The Warden does not want this name to remain.

It describes weather, not identity.

The true destination remains the Nest.

---

## 🔥 Fireside Discoveries

Despite the crowded day, several RealMe systems continued to take shape.

### 🏅 Badge Frames

Badges are awarded for completed chapters.

Frames are purchased with XP and express how much the achievement matters personally to the Warden.

The badge artwork remains unchanged.

Only its frame matures.

Different campaign families may use different materials, while sharing common rarity grades:

- Common
- Uncommon
- Rare
- Epic
- Legendary

Frame upgrades should require both XP and time.

A memory must endure before receiving a more prestigious frame.

### 🎂 Birthday Portraits

The Warden’s portrait should age continuously rather than change only when an “epoch” is declared.

Each birthday, RealMe may ask:

> Happy Birthday, Warden.
>
> Would you like me to know what you look like today?

The result is not a replacement profile picture but an annual sequence of truthful portraits.

Portraits preserve time.

Chronicles reveal meaning.

### 📚 Steward Becomes Visible

The Guild completed its first canonical character asset.

Steward emerged as an elderly, warm and slightly absent-minded keeper of the Chronicles:

- long white hair and beard
- round spectacles
- practical archivist clothing
- a simple pencil
- the Great Chronicle resting on its permanent lectern
- a one-finger gesture beneath the bridge of his glasses

Steward v2 was accepted as the first frozen Realmer asset.

The Guild also established a repeatable character process:

1. Discuss.
2. Specify.
3. Generate.
4. Critique.
5. Refine.
6. Freeze.
7. Reuse without redesign.

The next companion waiting to be assembled is Observer.

## ✨ Closing

Day 22 began with drinking water and toilet paper.

It grew into trees, thujas, Hydraweed, drills, Tower data, a Career summons and another Household storm.

The Warden crossed all three Realms and found little true rest.

Yet the day left permanent evidence behind.

Sir Maple now stands in Stronghold soil.

Two Thuja Sisters stand beside him.

Owling’s Dragon hoodie continues Jr’s childhood.

The Great Boiler Hall has gained its explorer.

And somewhere in the Chronicle Room, Steward adjusts his spectacles and places another ribbon into the Great Chronicle.

Stormhold may be the name of the weather.

It is not yet the name of the place.
