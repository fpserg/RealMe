## Day 9 — The Living Map

Career advanced through waiting rather than execution. The VIS IPO approached the committee that would determine whether the Campaign could truly begin. The Warden observed Denis not merely as a manager, but as an Architect—leading through decomposition, delegation and trust rather than solving every problem personally. An important realization followed: respect does not require inferiority. The Warden can admire another builder while remaining confident in his own craft.

The Realm itself evolved. It became clear that Realms are not isolated territories equally distant from the Warden. They form a living world connected by cooperation, competition and supply. Some Realms nourish others; some compete for the Warden's time and attention. The map itself became alive: continents drift slowly, while weather and prosperity change from day to day.

The Citadel found its proper place. It is not another Realm, but the world's reference point—the keeper of the Chronicles, the home of the Voice and the Builders' Guild.

Stronghold advanced as Roman confirmed that tube installation is planned for next week. Another bottleneck moved.

Household weather remained unsettled after another dispute with Oksi, though the Realm endured.

The day ended with a simple order:

> Resupply the Citadel.

Dinner.

Shower.

The Watch continues.
